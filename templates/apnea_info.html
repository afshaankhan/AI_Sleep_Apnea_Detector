😴 What is Sleep Apnea?

Sleep apnea is a common but potentially serious sleep disorder in which breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep. This disruption can lead to daytime fatigue, poor concentration, memory issues, and even increased risk of heart disease, stroke, and diabetes.

The two most common types are:

  • Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA): Caused by physical blockage of the airway.
  • Central Sleep Apnea (CSA): Caused by miscommunication between brain and breathing muscles.

🔍 Severity Scale (AHI)

Sleep apnea severity is clinically diagnosed using the Apnea-Hypopnea Index (AHI), which measures the number of breathing interruptions per hour.

AHISeverity
0–4Normal
5–14Mild
15–29Moderate
≥30Severe

🧪 How Does Our Model Detect It?

We divide the signal data into 30-second windows and analyze features like:

  • ECG/EEG/BP signal shape, frequency & entropy
  • Spectral energy, RMS, skew, kurtosis
  • Classification via ensemble machine learning models

If 5% or more of your signal windows are flagged as apneatic, our system alerts you with a possible diagnosis.

📌 What Should You Do If Flagged?

Don’t panic — this tool is meant as an awareness assistant, not a medical diagnosis. We recommend:

  • Scheduling a sleep study with a professional
  • Tracking your snoring and fatigue symptoms
  • Exploring lifestyle changes like weight management or sleep position
